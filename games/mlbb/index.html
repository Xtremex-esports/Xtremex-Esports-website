GPOW Elite's rise came out of setbacks, rebuilding, and a stronger reset that finally gave the MLBB side a real foundation to grow from.

Formation

XTremeX MLBB, also known as GPOW Elite, was formally formed on January 4, 2026. The division had already lived through earlier qualifier attempts from 2024, but the team chose that January 2026 reset as the point where the MLBB side truly became structured again.

The Story So Far

  • 2024 The MLBB side had already begun appearing in qualifiers by 2024, but that first stretch was anything but smooth. It was a difficult period marked by painful setbacks, failed runs, roster instability, and the frustration of watching progress break down just when the team needed momentum most. Players came in, played under the banner, and later left. There were also sabotage-related problems that made the journey even harder. Instead of building cleanly from one qualifier to the next, the team kept getting pulled backward, and the MLBB side remained unstable for a long time.
  • Rebuild Phase After enduring all those setbacks, the team made the decision to start fresh instead of forcing the old cycle to continue. That reset began with Gogo and Leroy, who became the foundation of the new direction. From there, the MLBB side did not grow overnight, but it began to expand with more intention, more seriousness, and a clearer sense of identity. This phase was about rebuilding trust, rebuilding structure, and giving the sector a better base to grow from after a rough and discouraging beginning.
  • January 2026 January 2026 became the real turning point. The recruitment of Gogo, Sunless, AlreadyPro, Igris, and Zuman marked the moment the rise truly began. That intake gave the team stronger balance, better energy, and a more serious competitive outlook. It was no longer just a side trying to recover from the past. This was the point where GPOW Elite started becoming something more organized, more ambitious, and more capable of building a lasting presence.
  • Early Team Growth Even after the team was formally formed, the MLBB side was still quite unpolished. Synergy was poor, coordination was inconsistent, and the roster was still learning how to function as a real unit. But through extra training, steady effort, and the guidance of Coach Edmund Bamfo, the team improved drastically. What began as a rough and uneven side started becoming more disciplined, more connected, and much more dangerous in competition.
  • Breakthrough Results That improvement translated into real results. GPOW Elite went on to place second in the EAG National Qualifiers for Ghana, a major sign of how far the squad had come from its unstable early phase. On top of that, three of the team's players earned places in the Ghana National Team, showing that the project was not only growing as a unit, but also producing players capable of reaching the national level.

Coach

Through the chaos, the resets, and the difficult years, Coach Edmund Bamfo remained the steady hand behind the MLBB side. He stayed through the ups and downs, kept belief in the squad, and provided the structure that helped the team rebuild with purpose. His leadership has been one of the clearest reasons the rise of GPOW Elite became possible.
